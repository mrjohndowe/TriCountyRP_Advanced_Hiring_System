# Interviewer Handbook

This handbook is the standard reference for anyone conducting interviews within Tri County RP.

## Core Principles

- Be consistent
- Be respectful
- Be objective
- Be attentive
- Be thorough

## Interviewer Roles

### Lead Interviewer
- Opens and directs the interview
- Controls pacing
- Asks the primary questions
- Handles the closing statement

### Secondary Interviewer
- Asks follow-up questions
- Probes for detail
- Watches for gaps in the applicant's answers

### Note Taker / Reviewer
- Records key answers
- Tracks attitude, maturity, and clarity
- Helps summarize strengths and concerns

## What To Look For

### Strong Signs
- Calm and clear communication
- Real understanding of roleplay etiquette
- Accountability and maturity
- Respect toward other members
- Willingness to learn
- Realistic activity commitment

### Warning Signs
- Arrogance
- Vague or contradictory answers
- Focus on rank instead of service
- Poor understanding of server professionalism
- Unwillingness to accept feedback
- History of drama-heavy behavior

## Follow-Up Question Examples

- Can you explain why you would choose that approach?
- How would you handle that if another staff member disagreed?
- What would you do differently if the scene became more chaotic?
- How would you document or report that issue after the scene?

## Interviewing Rules

Do:
- Let applicants finish speaking
- Ask for examples when answers are too broad
- Write clear notes
- Remain neutral

Do Not:
- Mock, pressure, or embarrass the applicant
- Promise acceptance
- Ask irrelevant personal questions
- Allow favoritism to influence scoring
